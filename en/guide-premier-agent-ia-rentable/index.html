How to identify your first profitable AI Agent.

The right starting point is not technology. It is a repetitive task that costs time, money or energy every week.

1. Start from visible friction

Look for a task you postpone, repeat often or delegate poorly: follow-ups, qualification, summaries, sorting, reply preparation.

If nobody feels the friction, the AI Agent may become a gadget.

2. Choose a precise output

A good AI Agent does not â€œdo AIâ€. It produces a clear output: a brief, a message, a checklist, a summary or a draft to approve.

The more concrete the deliverable, the easier adoption becomes.

3. Measure before building

Before building, estimate time lost, task frequency and business impact.

The first AI Agent should be small, useful and quickly testable.
